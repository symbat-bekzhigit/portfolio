By meticulously curating content and design, I endeavor to create an immersive experience that resonates deeply with viewers. Guess The Sound

HTML, CSS, JavaScript

The sound project was inspired by the social media trends of ASMR and Mukbangs to depict an engaging online experience akin to a TV game show. Typically ASMR represents trying to come up with different sounds to make the listener feel a ticklish feeling, while Mukbangs are generally eating on camera. The team wanted to combine the audio-entertaining components of both to create the “Late Night Guess the Sound” quiz. Through creative design and development, the website showcases an exciting sound guessing game having the players immerse themselves in a variety of audio challenges, where their keen ears are put to the test. Each round presents a diverse array of sounds and expects participants to decipher their origins. With each correct guess, players earn points. Structured into two distinct question types, the game offers variety and complexity. Type 1 questions consist of multiple-choice options, asking to guess the identity of a single sound. Meanwhile, Type 2 questions elevate the stakes, presenting similar sounds alongside a keyword, challenging players to discern the most fitting match. With only accurate answers contributing to their cumulative score, players are pushed to guess correctly to get the most points.

This was a team project. Other contributors are Tomiris Suleimenova, Khadija Khalid, and Sara Alameri.

Double Delight

HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Adobe Premiere Pro, Adobe After Effects

This project aims to establish an innovative video channel to engage audiences to learn how to cook effectively via interactive video tutorials. Following the example of the picturesque, low-fi, faceless cooking videos on YouTube and Instagram, we shot our videos to emphasise a peaceful visual style, which is characterised with soft light and body language as an expressive element. The website focuses on using one general cake mix and subsequently, through different video routes, it teaches users to make two separate recipes. Viewers are guided to pick their base recipe from pre-selected options, and are then invited to customise their creation by using the various decorative options that are displayed later on. The aim was to create an immersive and interactive experience so the videos not only teach but also entertain, offering relaxing visuals and stimulating audience participation during the process.

This was a team project. Other contributors are Khadija Khalid, Mariam Al Khoori, and Faizan Raza.
